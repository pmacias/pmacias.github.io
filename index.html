postdoctoral

NASA Roman Space Telescope

As a postdoctoral researcher in the Astronomy and Astrophysics Department at UC Santa Cruz, I had the privilege of helping design the High Latitude Time Domain Survey (HLTDS) for the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope (Roman), a NASA flagship mission set to launch in August 2026.

I served as a member of a Science Investigation Team, creating and analyzing simulated "observations" to ensure our survey strategy met NASA dark-energy requirements. The technical report we produced served as the basis for the strategy that will be employed once the HLTDS begins in late 2028! Learn more about Roman at the link below.

Previous Research

My PhD work consisted of two tenuously-related topics: the hydrodynamics of interacting stars, and the origin of the heaviest elements in the Universe. The former involved running large-scale hydro simulations on supercomputers, and the latter entailed building a physics-based analytical model to reanalyze existing astronomical data.
